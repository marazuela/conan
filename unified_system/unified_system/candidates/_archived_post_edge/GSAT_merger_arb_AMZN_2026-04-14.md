## Thesis

Large strategic acquisition with:
- **Deal risk level**: MODERATE — not just because of size, but because of the satellite milestone contingency (a first-party execution risk unique to this deal) and FCC/DOJ scrutiny given spectrum concentration.
- **Shareholder approval**: essentially locked (majority written consent obtained simultaneously with signing — a commonly-used structure where a major holder pre-commits; here likely Apple's ~20% stake plus insider / strategic holders suffices for majority).
- **Regulatory risk (novel angle, worth monitoring)**: Amazon acquiring satellite + Apple retaining D2D service commitment creates an Amazon-Apple-GSAT tripartite arrangement that could draw DOJ attention, particularly given space/satellite-broadband concentration concerns around Starlink, Amazon Leo (Kuiper successor), and now a consolidated D2D spectrum holder.

**Non-obvious angle**: The **0.3210 AMZN share alternative is collar-less above $90/share**. If AMZN appreciates >$280/share by close, the stock election is capped at $90 value — i.e., stockholders don't benefit from AMZN appreciation above that level. This creates a subtle short-AMZN embedded hedge for anyone taking stock consideration. Conversely, if AMZN declines, the stock option becomes worth less than $90 → proration forces more cash election → 40% cash cap kicks in → stockholders forcibly take stock at below $90 value. So stock consideration holders have **asymmetric downside** (AMZN downside) with **capped upside** ($90 ceiling). This makes the cash election dominant strategy for rational holders → cash proration cap will almost certainly bind.

**Implication**: Effective per-share consideration = blend of 40% cash ($90) + 60% stock worth whatever 0.321 AMZN is at close. Currently AMZN at [to verify], so stock leg value ≈ 0.321 × current AMZN price (if capped, =$90). **We need AMZN's current price to compute expected blended value, and to monitor blended value over time vs. GSAT price.**

**Interpretation**: This is standard CFO compensation mechanics, not pre-merger flight. An insider selling ahead of a merger close would typically (a) sell a meaningful % of holdings, not the tax-withholding portion of a fresh grant; (b) sell open-market not via 144 filing on vest; (c) hold materially less than the ~$9.3M gross-value equity at post-vest prices. Clary's residual 115,741 shares × $80.64 = ~$9.33M still aligned to deal close. **No thesis change. No scoring change.**

**New monitoring rule**: For merger-arb candidates with long-dated close, distinguish tax-withholding Form 144s (acquisition nature = "Restricted Stock Vesting" or "Exercise") from voluntary insider sales (acquisition nature = "Open Market Purchase" or "Private Purchase"). Only the latter are thesis signals. Add to `SESSION_STATE.md` warning #30 as a sub-pattern.
