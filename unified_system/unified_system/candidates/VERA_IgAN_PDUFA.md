## Executive Summary

Vera Therapeutics (VERA, $3.2B mcap, ~71.7M shares) has a BLA under FDA Priority Review for **atacicept** — the first dual BAFF+APRIL inhibitor — for IgA nephropathy (IgAN). PDUFA **July 7, 2026**. Priority Review + Breakthrough Therapy Designation + Accelerated Approval pathway. Phase 3 ORIGIN data are exceptional and were published in the New England Journal of Medicine: 46% proteinuria reduction from baseline, 42% reduction vs placebo, p<0.0001 at week 36, plus 68% Gd-IgA1 reduction and resolution of hematuria in 81% of patients with baseline hematuria. These effect sizes are at or above the best data in the IgAN class.

The central tension is **commercial positioning**, not approvability: four IgAN therapies are already FDA-approved (sparsentan/Filspari, iptacopan/Fabhalta, atrasentan/Vanrafia, sibeprenlimab) plus Tarpeyo budesonide. **Sibeprenlimab** (Visterra/Otsuka, APRIL-only) was approved **November 25, 2025** at $292,500 annual net price — the most mechanistically adjacent competitor and the direct reference point for atacicept pricing. Atacicept's dual BAFF+APRIL mechanism is a real differentiation but not a blowout.

**Thesis**: PDUFA approval probability is high (NEJM data, BTD, Priority Review, AdCom not required, Accelerated Approval track, no CMC flags, recent Point72 and Deep Track 13G filings signal institutional conviction, ex-Citi biotech banker Chris Hite joined the board March 5). The asymmetry is not the approval — it is the **commercial positioning gap** the market may be underestimating: atacicept's superior proteinuria effect size and lower cost potential versus sibeprenlimab's $292.5K sticker could reset IgAN market share expectations post-label.

## Thesis Statement

**What the market sees**: Another IgAN drug in a crowded field, behind sibeprenlimab (APRIL-only, approved Nov 2025, $292.5K/yr), iptacopan, sparsentan, atrasentan, budesonide. Consensus models a modest commercial trajectory and values VERA primarily on the binary of FDA approval.

**What the market is missing**: The **shape and magnitude** of atacicept's effect in ORIGIN — 42% placebo-adjusted proteinuria reduction plus 68% Gd-IgA1 reduction plus 81% hematuria resolution — is the most complete efficacy package in the class. The dual BAFF+APRIL mechanism addresses the upstream pathology (Gd-IgA1 production) more directly than APRIL-alone sibeprenlimab or complement-pathway iptacopan. If clinical uptake follows proteinuria effect size rather than first-mover advantage, atacicept can take meaningful share from sibeprenlimab even though it launches second. Pricing pressure from sibeprenlimab's $292.5K benchmark is real, but that ceiling is also the floor: any sellside model that has assumed VERA would price below standard-of-care will be wrong if Vera prices at parity.

**What has to be true for the thesis**:
1. FDA approves on July 7, 2026 (base case — high probability given data, BTD, no AdCom, Priority Review, no CMC issues, Accelerated Approval track)
2. Label includes all comers IgAN (not restricted to post-SoC-failure)
3. Commercial launch executes on schedule (mid-2026)
4. Proteinuria effect size translates to KOL-preferred positioning at nephrology centers
