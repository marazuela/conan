## Thesis

Travere Therapeutics faces an FDA decision on April 13, 2026 for the supplemental NDA (sNDA) seeking traditional approval of FILSPARI (sparsentan) for focal segmental glomerulosclerosis (FSGS). If approved, FILSPARI would be the **first and only FDA-approved treatment for FSGS**, a rare kidney disease that is a leading cause of kidney failure. FILSPARI is already approved for IgA nephropathy and has demonstrated benefit in FSGS through its Phase 3 DUPLEX trial.

The market appears to be pricing moderate probability of approval. The stock dropped ~18.7% when the FDA extended the PDUFA from January 13 to April 13 due to a "Major Amendment" — but critically, the extension was for additional clinical benefit characterization, **not safety or manufacturing issues**. The FDA also bypassed an advisory committee (AdCom), which historically correlates with higher approval probability for drugs where the agency is already confident in the data. The sNDA has Priority Review designation.

The key risk is that FSGS is notoriously difficult to study — the DUPLEX trial's primary endpoint (proteinuria reduction) showed benefit, but the FDA's request for additional characterization of clinical benefit suggests scrutiny around the surrogate-to-clinical-outcome linkage. CEO Eric Dube sold 60,000 shares on April 1 via a pre-planned 10b5-1 arrangement (set up in June 2025), which is not a discretionary sell signal. Meanwhile, Palisades Investment Partners purchased 137,768 shares around the same time.
