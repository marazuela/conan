## 3. Clinical Evidence Base

**AXS-05** is a fixed-dose combination of dextromethorphan (an uncompetitive NMDA receptor antagonist and sigma-1 agonist) and bupropion (a CYP2D6 inhibitor that boosts dextromethorphan plasma concentrations and a weak NDRI itself). Already FDA-approved as Auvelity for major depressive disorder (2022).

### ADA Phase 3 Program — 4 Trials

| Trial | Design | Result on CMAI | Status |
|-------|--------|----------------|--------|
| **ADVANCE-1** | RCT, AXS-05 vs placebo, ~400 pts, 5 weeks | **POSITIVE** — stat-sig reduction vs placebo | Topline reported 2022 |
| **ADVANCE-2** | RCT, 408 pts | **MISSED primary** — 13.8 vs 12.6 placebo (small placebo delta) | Topline reported 2023 |
| **ACCORD-1** | Relapse prevention, responder randomization | **POSITIVE** — stat-sig longer time to relapse | Published Neurology 2024 |
| **ACCORD-2** | Second relapse prevention trial | **POSITIVE** — presented AAN 2025 | Apr 2025 |

**Hit rate: 3 of 4 on primary endpoints.** The FDA has approved drugs on less — lecanemab was approved on 1 positive Phase 3 (CLARITY-AD). What matters for the review division is:
1. Do the positive trials triangulate a consistent effect size? (Yes — ADVANCE-1, ACCORD-1, ACCORD-2 all significant)
2. Is the miss explainable? (ADVANCE-2 had an unusually large placebo response — 12.6 point reduction vs. placebo norms of ~8–10; this is a known issue in agitation trials)
3. Is the effect clinically meaningful? (Cohen's d effect sizes in positive trials were moderate, clinically meaningful per FDA agitation guidance)

The "mixed program" framing (Session 24 inherited) was incorrect framing. A 3-of-4 hit rate with 2 relapse-prevention trials both positive is a stronger package than typical.

### Safety — The Real Review Question

The FDA's primary concern in an ADA label is whether a new drug will increase:
- Mortality (brexpiprazole had to address this)
- Sedation/falls (fragile elderly population)
- Cognitive decline (confound the underlying disease)
- Cerebrovascular events (antipsychotics in dementia have boxed warnings)

**AXS-05's advantage:** It is NOT an antipsychotic. The mechanism (dextromethorphan + bupropion) has been in humans for decades with well-characterized safety. The MDD commercial dataset is large (>100K patient-years). FDA has already negotiated the MDD label.

**Risks to thesis:** The elderly AD population has different pharmacokinetics, and bupropion seizure risk may be elevated in frail elderly. But this is a dose issue, not a mechanism issue — addressable with labeling.
