## Signal Validation: Pre-Positioning as Leading Indicator

**The Apr 8 -24.6% crash on 2x volume was the market correctly pre-positioning for the CRL outcome 2 trading days before the decision.** This is the key empirical validation from this PDUFA:

1. **Market efficiency on biotech binary events**: When a high-profile PDUFA catalyst is preceded by a sharp, volume-confirmed decline with no discrete news trigger, the implied probability of an unfavorable outcome is elevated enough that the signal should be treated as **high-conviction**, not as a potential entry.
2. **Decoupling as confirmation**: TVTX rallied +12.61% 10d while REPL crashed -34.1% — complete divergence despite both being FDA-dependent. This decoupling was a second confirmation the REPL-specific fear was not sector-wide.
3. **Second-CRL base rate**: RP1 had a prior CRL. Second-CRL rate on re-submissions with lingering data adequacy concerns (not manufacturing-only) is substantially higher than first-cycle baseline. The market appears to have re-priced this between Apr 6 and Apr 8.

## Methodology Lessons (Candidate for DECISIONS.md)

**Proposed rule D-037 (draft):** "Pre-PDUFA price action weighting"
- When a candidate with a PDUFA catalyst within ≤5 trading days experiences a single-day decline ≥15% on volume ≥1.5× 20-day average, **without a discrete adverse news trigger**, treat this as a strong leading signal that market-internal information (insider selling, options flow, hedge-fund positioning) is pricing an unfavorable outcome.
- Specifically: **do not score the pre-PDUFA weakness as a dip-buying opportunity**. Instead, weight it as kill-condition evidence for any existing long thesis on the same catalyst, and downgrade the candidate immediately.
