## Thesis

MannKind Corporation's Afrezza (inhaled insulin) pediatric sBLA represents the first needle-free insulin option for children aged 4-17 with Type 1 or Type 2 diabetes. If approved, it addresses a massive unmet need — children who fear injections, leading to poor adherence and glycemic control. Analyst consensus is Buy with average PT $8.64 (236% upside from $2.57).

## Critical Risk: ITT vs mITT Noninferiority

**This is the key FDA review question.** The Phase 3 INHALE-1 study had a mixed result on the primary endpoint:

- **ITT (full population)**: HbA1c difference exceeded the 0.4% noninferiority margin (0.435%). **TECHNICALLY MISSED.**
- **mITT (excluding one non-adherent patient)**: HbA1c difference was 0.370%, within the 0.4% margin. **PASSED.**

The FDA's standard approach is to use ITT as the primary analysis. MannKind is arguing that mITT should be the primary basis because the ITT miss was driven by a single protocol-non-adherent patient. The FDA accepted the sBLA for review, which is a positive signal (they could have refused to file), but acceptance is not approval.

**Risk assessment**: This is a 40-60 proposition. The FDA has accepted mITT arguments before (particularly when the ITT miss is clearly attributable to a single outlier), but they've also rejected them. The fact that the margin was exceeded by only 0.035% in ITT (0.435% vs 0.400% threshold) is both a positive (very close) and a negative (it's still technically a miss).
