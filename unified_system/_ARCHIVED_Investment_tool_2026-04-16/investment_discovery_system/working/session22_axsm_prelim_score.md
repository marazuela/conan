## Thesis framing

AXS-05 is already approved for MDD as Auvelity. The sNDA is for Alzheimer's Disease agitation — a TAM of ~5 million US patients where the only approved treatment is Rexulti (brexpiprazole, Otsuka/Lundbeck) with a boxed warning. If approved, AXS-05 would have a cleaner safety profile (no antipsychotic class warnings) and could take material share, supporting bull-case $200-300 PTs at AXSM.

## The problem — mixed Phase 3 package

Axsome's pivotal program had three trials:
- **ADVANCE-1**: Positive on primary endpoint (CMAI reduction)
- **ADVANCE-2**: **FAILED** — numerical but not statistically significant improvement vs placebo (CMAI −13.8 AXS-05 vs −12.6 placebo)
- **ACCORD**: Positive on relapse-prevention endpoint (hazard ratio 0.276, p=0.014)

The FDA has historically been cautious about relapse-prevention designs as stand-alone substantial evidence. The agency will need to conclude that ADVANCE-1 + ACCORD together satisfy the "two adequate and well-controlled trials" bar despite ADVANCE-2 failing. Under the current FDA regime (Makary, Prasad-era CDER oversight), the bar for approving a second indication on an imperfect pivotal package has tightened. See REPL IGNYTE CRL precedent (July 2025) where the FDA rejected a positive trial as "not adequate and well-controlled."
