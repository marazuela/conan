## Drug: Relutrigine (NDA, Priority Review, Breakthrough Therapy)

### Indication
SCN2A and SCN8A developmental and epileptic encephalopathies (DEEs) — severe pediatric epilepsy syndromes caused by sodium channel mutations.

### Clinical Data
- **EMBOLD Study**: Stopped early for efficacy by Data Monitoring Committee (Nov 2025)
- **Placebo-adjusted monthly motor seizure reduction**: 46% during 16-week double-blind period
- **Seizure freedom**: >30% of patients on relutrigine
- **Open-label extension**: ~90% seizure reduction, >2 months between seizures
- **Breakthrough Therapy Designation**: Granted
- **Priority Review**: Granted (NDA accepted Mar 30, 2026)

### Market Opportunity
- US market potential: $3B+ (based on >200,000 target patients)
- Peak revenue: Company estimates >$5B across relutrigine indications
- EMERALD study in broader DEEs: enrolling, expected fully enrolled H2 2026

### Competition
- First therapy specifically designed for SCN2A/SCN8A DEEs
- No direct competitors in this specific indication
- Company also has elsunersen (ASO) for SCN2A — viewed as complementary, not cannibalizing

## Why PRAX Is NOT a Candidate Despite Near-Certain Approval

1. **No information asymmetry**: 18 analysts, Strong Buy consensus, $8.6B market cap. The approval is fully priced in. Our edge is zero.

2. **Limited risk/reward**: At $310 with PT $374-511, the upside from approval is 20-65%. For a binary event where CRL (however unlikely) could mean 50%+ downside, the expected value is marginal. Compare to TVTX (54% upside with more uncertainty) or VRDN (178% upside with significant dislocation).

3. **The system's edge is finding mispricing, not picking winners**: PRAX will very likely be approved. But the stock already reflects that. Our pipeline generates value by finding situations where the market has MISPRICED the probability or magnitude of a catalyst — not by confirming what everyone already knows.

4. **Contrast with VRDN (31.5)**: VRDN has similar approval probability for veligrotug but trades at $14 vs PT $39 (178% upside) because unrelated factors (elegrobart, Amgen) crushed the stock. That's a dislocation. PRAX has no comparable dislocation.
