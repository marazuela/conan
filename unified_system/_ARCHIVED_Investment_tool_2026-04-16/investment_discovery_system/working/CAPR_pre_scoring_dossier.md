## Drug: Deramiocel (CAP-1002)

### Indication
Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy (DMD) Cardiomyopathy — DMD is a fatal X-linked genetic disorder causing progressive muscle weakness. Cardiomyopathy is the leading cause of death in DMD. No approved therapy specifically targets DMD cardiomyopathy.

### Mechanism
Deramiocel is an allogeneic cardiosphere-derived cell (CDC) therapy. CDCs are cardiac progenitor cells that exert anti-fibrotic, anti-inflammatory, and regenerative effects. Administered IV every 3 months.

### Regulatory Status
- **BLA originally filed**: 2025
- **CRL received**: July 2025 (FDA requested additional data)
- **BLA resubmitted**: Early 2026 with HOPE-3 data
- **Class 2 resubmission** (6-month review)
- **PDUFA**: August 22, 2026
- **Designations**: Orphan Drug, Rare Pediatric Disease (PRV eligible)
- **PRV potential**: If approved, Capricor expects to receive a Priority Review Voucher (~$100-150M value)

### Clinical Data (HOPE-3 Phase 3)
- **Design**: Multicenter, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled. 106 patients (ambulatory and non-ambulatory). 4 doses q3 months over 12 months.
- **Primary endpoint MET**: PUL v2.0 (Performance of Upper Limb) — 54% slowing of progression vs placebo (statistically significant)
- **Key secondary cardiac endpoint MET**: LVEF — 91% slowing of cardiac decline. In cardiomyopathy subgroup: +3.3 pp improvement vs placebo (p=0.017)
- **Myocardial fibrosis** (LGE): 3-segment treatment difference (p=0.022)
- **All Type I error-controlled secondary endpoints met**
- **Safety**: Favorable profile, consistent with prior experience
- **First-in-class**: No approved therapy for DMD cardiomyopathy

### Key Strengths
- First-in-class cell therapy for DMD cardiomyopathy (leading cause of DMD death)
- Phase 3 met primary and ALL key secondary endpoints with statistical significance
- 91% attenuation of cardiac decline is dramatic
- PRV eligibility adds ~$100-150M value on approval
- Strong unmet medical need (fatal disease, no approved cardiac therapy)
- Orphan drug pricing potential

### Key Risks
- **Prior CRL** — FDA already rejected once. Resubmission ≠ guaranteed approval (~60-70% probability vs ~80-90% for fresh BLAs)
- **Cell therapy manufacturing complexity** — allogeneic CDCs require specialized manufacturing and logistics
- **Stock already up 500%+ from CRL low** — massive move already captured approval expectations
- **Small patient population** — DMD cardiomyopathy is rare (est. 15,000-20,000 DMD patients in US, subset with cardiomyopathy)
- **1 Sell rating** among 10 analysts — someone disagrees
