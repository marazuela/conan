## 2. Clinical Data — CRITICAL NUANCE

### VERITAC-2 Phase 3 Results (Topline March 11, 2025):

**ESR1-mutant subgroup (primary endpoint region)**:
- Median PFS: **5.0 months vepdegestrant vs. 2.1 months fulvestrant**
- HR 0.57 (95% CI 0.42-0.77), p<0.001
- 43% risk reduction
- ✅ **STATISTICALLY SIGNIFICANT and clinically meaningful**

**ITT population (secondary endpoint — but crucial for broader label)**:
- Median PFS: **3.7 months vepdegestrant vs. 3.6 months fulvestrant**
- HR 0.83 (95% CI 0.68-1.02), p=0.07
- ❌ **FAILED** to reach statistical significance

**Overall survival**: Immature at topline. Secondary endpoint.

**Safety**: Generally well tolerated, consistent with prior studies. No new safety signals.

### What this means for FDA review:

The 2.9-month PFS improvement in ESR1m is strong. The ~0.1-month difference in ITT is a near-complete wash.

**Most likely FDA outcome**: Approval restricted to ESR1m population only. This is consistent with elacestrant (Menarini's Orserdu), which got the same narrow label in Jan 2023.

**Risk**: FDA could require more data on ITT before approval, extend PDUFA, or issue CRL. Low probability given ESR1m data is clean, but non-zero.

**Upside ceiling**: A broader ITT label is essentially impossible given the p=0.07. This caps commercial ceiling significantly vs. initial expectations.

## 3. Commercial Context
