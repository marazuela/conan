## Drug: Cytisinicline
- **Mechanism**: Plant-derived alkaloid, partial agonist at nicotinic acetylcholine receptors (same target as Chantix/varenicline)
- **Indication**: Nicotine dependence for smoking cessation (NDA submitted). Also: vaping cessation (FDA CNPV awarded, earlier stage)
- **If approved**: First new smoking cessation treatment in ~20 years. Also potential first-ever vaping cessation treatment.

---

## Clinical Data

### ORCA-2 (Phase 3, n=810)
- **Primary endpoint MET**: Both 6-week and 12-week cytisinicline arms showed significantly higher quit rates vs placebo
- **Odds ratios**: 8.0 (6-week) and 6.3 (12-week) vs placebo
- **Safety**: Only 2.9% discontinued due to adverse events. No drug-related SAEs.
- **Published**: JAMA (top-tier journal)

### ORCA-3 (Phase 3, n=792, confirmatory)
- **Primary endpoint MET**: 12-week cytisinicline → 30.3% quit rate (weeks 9-12) vs 9.4% placebo
- **Odds ratio**: 4.4× higher odds of quitting
- **Continuous abstinence (weeks 9-24)**: 20.5% cytisinicline vs 4.2% placebo
- **Published**: JAMA Internal Medicine

### ORCA-V1 (Phase 2, vaping cessation)
- **Participants**: 2.6× more likely to quit vaping vs placebo
- **Published**: JAMA Internal Medicine

## Competitive Landscape
- **Chantix (varenicline, Pfizer)**: Hit $1B+ annual sales. Voluntarily recalled (2021) due to nitrosamine contamination. FDA label carries suicidality warning. Cytisinicline has 4× lower nausea rate.
- **Zyban (bupropion, GSK)**: Antidepressant repurposed. Less effective than varenicline.
- **NRT (patches, gums)**: OTC, modest efficacy. Cytisinicline is prescription but more effective.
- **Key advantage**: Cytisinicline may be safer than Chantix AND more effective than OTC options — potential to capture middle ground.
- **ICER assessment**: Not adequate evidence that cytisinicline net health benefit > varenicline. But safety profile (less nausea, no suicidality signal) is a practical prescribing advantage.
