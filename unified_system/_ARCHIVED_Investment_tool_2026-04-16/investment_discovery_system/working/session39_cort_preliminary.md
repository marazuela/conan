## ROSELLA Phase 3 Key Data

- **N**: 381 patients, platinum-resistant ovarian cancer
- **Design**: Relacorilant + nab-paclitaxel vs. nab-paclitaxel alone, 1:1 randomization
- **Primary PFS**: HR 0.70, p=0.008 — 30% reduction in progression risk
- **Primary OS**: HR 0.65, p=0.0004 — 35% reduction in death risk
- **Published**: The Lancet (March 2026); first presented ASCO 2025
- **ESMO 2025 late-breaker**: Demonstrated benefit in prior PARP inhibitor patients (key subgroup)

## Risk Assessment

### Bull Case
- Exceptional Phase 3 results (both PFS and OS, Lancet-published)
- No AdCom announced
- Broad label (no biomarker)
- PROC is a high unmet need area with poor prognosis
- Short squeeze potential on approval
- CIK clean: No adverse 8-K filings since sNDA acceptance

### Bear Case
- Corcept already RECEIVED a CRL for relacorilant in HYPERCORTISOLISM (Dec 2025) — different indication, different data package, but same molecule. The CRL may be creating an overhang of skepticism.
- Insider selling: Multiple Form 4 and 144 filings in Mar-Apr 2026. Need to investigate whether these are routine or unusually heavy.
- The ovarian cancer market is crowded (PARP inhibitors, bevacizumab, mirvetuximab soravtansine/Elahere).
- Price recovery from CRL-driven selloff may have already happened.

### Kill Conditions (Proposed)
1. CRL for ovarian cancer indication
2. FDA AdCom announced
3. PDUFA extension
4. Safety signal in ROSELLA follow-up data
5. Major insider selling acceleration
6. NDA withdrawal
