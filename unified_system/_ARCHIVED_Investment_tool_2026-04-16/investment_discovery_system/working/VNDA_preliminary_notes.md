> **Drug**: Imsidolimab (IL-36R antagonist) for Generalized Pustular Psoriasis (GPP)

## Why This May Be Worth Scoring

- **106% upside**: $7.45 stock vs avg PT $14.9 (5 analysts, Strong Buy)
- **Revenue company**: $216M revenue (2025), guiding $230-260M (2026). NOT pre-revenue.
- **4 commercial products**: Fanapt (bipolar/schizophrenia), HETLIOZ (sleep), PONVORY (MS), NEREUS (motion sickness)
- **NEREUS just approved**: First oral motion sickness drug in 40 years. GLP-1 vomiting Phase 3 planned H1 2026.
- **$430M market cap**: Modest for a revenue-generating company — may reflect market skepticism

## Imsidolimab Clinical Data

- **GEMINI-1**: 53% GPPPGA 0/1 at week 4 vs 13% placebo (p=0.0131). Both 750mg and 300mg arms hit.
- **GEMINI-2**: 100% maintenance (all 8 SC imsidolimab patients stayed at GPPPGA 0/1, zero flares)
- **Safety**: Favorable profile, no clinically meaningful signals
- **Small trial size**: 45 patients in GEMINI-1, 16 in GEMINI-2 — FDA may scrutinize

## Competitive Landscape (Check at T-60)

- Spesolimab (Spevigo, Boehringer) already approved for GPP flares — imsidolimab would need differentiation
- IL-36 pathway is validated (both drugs target it differently)
- GPP is ultra-rare (~3,000-5,000 US patients) — small market

## Preliminary Concerns

- Small trial sizes (n=45 GEMINI-1, n=16 GEMINI-2) — FDA could request more data
- GPP is ultra-rare — commercial revenue potential limited
- Spesolimab already approved — imsidolimab may face competitive headwinds
- Standard review (not Priority) — FDA less urgency
